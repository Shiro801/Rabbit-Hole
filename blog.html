My Regional Schools Press Conference Experience

The Journey Begins

The excitement was palpable as we geared up for the Regional Schools Press Conference (RSPC) – our first major competition since the pandemic. Months of rigorous training with professional trainers had led up to this moment, and the anticipation was mixed with a fair amount of nerves.

The Preparation

Preparing for the RSPC was no small feat. We trained for months, honing our skills and learning new techniques. The dedication of our trainers, who pushed us to our limits, was instrumental in our preparation. This intense training regime was necessary, especially since it was our first time competing at such a level post-pandemic.

A Unique Workshop

One of the standout moments of the conference was a workshop with Kaye Brier, one of the first trans women journalists in the region. Her teaching style was unlike anything we had experienced before. Kaye's unique approach and perspective not only inspired us but also broadened our understanding of journalism in a profound way. Her session was a highlight and left a lasting impression on me.

The Competition

The atmosphere at the conference was electric, but it was also incredibly stressful. Technical difficulties plagued us, particularly when we discovered that one of our laptops wasn’t sealed. This, coupled with the stringent time limits, added an extra layer of pressure. Despite these challenges, we persevered, drawing on the months of preparation and support from our trainers and teammates.

A Moment of Relaxation

After the competition, we had the chance to visit SM and Ayala. This was a welcome break and a moment to unwind. It felt less stressful, allowing us to reflect on our journey and enjoy a bit of normalcy after the high-stress environment of the conference. These moments of relaxation were essential for recharging our spirits.

Looking Ahead

The RSPC experience was a significant milestone in my journey as a writer. It taught me the value of hard work, resilience, and the importance of preparation. Moving forward, I am committed to working even harder and training more seriously. This experience has ignited a new level of determination in me, and I am excited to apply the lessons learned as I continue to grow and develop my skills.

Reflections

Reflecting on my RSPC experience, I realize how much I have grown both as a writer and as a person. The challenges we faced, the unique learning opportunities, and the moments of camaraderie have all contributed to a richer, more nuanced perspective on journalism and competition. As I look to the future, I carry these lessons with me, ready to tackle new challenges with renewed vigor and passion.
